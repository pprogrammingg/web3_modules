Background — homomorphic encryption (FHE)

Not required for ZK rollup interviews, but you will hear it in the same sentence as “private chains.” Homomorphic encryption lets certain computations run on ciphertexts so that, after decryption, the result matches what you would have obtained on the plaintexts. Fully homomorphic schemes support general circuits (with noise management / bootstrapping); leveled schemes cap depth.

How it differs from ZK here: ZK proofs convince a verifier that a computation obeyed rules, often without revealing inputs—the emphasis is integrity / succinct verification. FHE emphasizes confidentiality during outsourced compute on encrypted data. SNARKs do not magically give you FHE; product teams sometimes conflate the two in slides.

On-chain reality: FHE today is usually too slow and too key-heavy for L1 execution; you may see hybrids (MPC, TEEs, narrow homomorphic ops). Treat homomorphic claims like any other: ask for threat model, latency, who holds keys, and failure modes. Glossary: homomorphic encryption.
